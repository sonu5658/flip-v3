The temperature of an object, usually measured in degrees-Fahrenheit or degrees-Celsius, tells us how much heat, or energy, the object has. A boiling cup of water has very active molecules moving around very quickly and producing the heat we feel on our hands and faces. Colder objects don't have as much energy .
